As a Security Analyst for Apollo, an e-commerce company, my first task was to understand the fundamentals of cybersecurity, identify potential threats, and learn how organizations protect their data and systems.

---

## What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or theft.

Organizations implement cybersecurity controls to protect:

* Customer information
* Financial records
* Business operations
* Intellectual property
* Company reputation

---

## Levels of Protection

Cybersecurity protections are implemented at multiple levels to help defend individuals, organizations, and nations from cyber threats.

### 1. Individual Level

Protecting personal devices, accounts, and information from cybercriminals.

Examples:

* Strong passwords
* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Antivirus software
* Safe browsing practices

### 2. Organizational Level

Organizations implement security controls to protect business operations, employees, customers, and sensitive data.

Examples:

* Security policies
* Firewalls
* Employee awareness training
* Access control systems

### 3. Government Level

Governments protect national infrastructure, public services, and sensitive information from cyber attacks.

Examples:

* National cybersecurity strategies
* Cyber defense agencies
* Critical infrastructure protection
* Cybercrime legislation

---

## Who Wants My Data?

Various entities collect and use personal data for different purposes.

### Internet Service Providers (ISPs)

* Monitor internet traffic
* Maintain network services
* May collect browsing information

### Advertisers

* Track online behavior
* Build user profiles
* Deliver targeted advertisements

### Search Engines

* Store search history
* Personalize search results
* Analyze user interests

### Social Networking Platforms

* Collect user activity and interactions
* Build detailed user profiles
* Generate targeted content and advertisements

### Websites and Cookies

* Store user preferences
* Track browsing sessions
* Analyze website usage

---

## Types of Organizational Data

### Traditional Data

Structured business information such as:

* Customer records
* Employee information
* Financial data
* Sales reports
* Inventory records

### IoT and Big Data

Large volumes of data generated from connected devices and modern systems.

Examples:

* Smart sensors
* Mobile devices
* E-commerce analytics
* Customer behavior tracking
* Cloud-based applications

## McCumber Cube (1991)/ The Cube

The McCumber Cube is a cybersecurity framework developed by John McCumber to help organizations design and evaluate information security by looking at data from multiple perspectives.

It is based on **3 dimensions**:

---

## 1. Security Principles (CIA Triad)

### Confidentiality

Ensures information is only accessible to authorized users.

Methods:

* Encryption
* MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)
* Identity verification

### Integrity

Ensures data is accurate and not altered without authorization.

Methods:

* Hashing
* Checksums

### Availability

Ensures systems and data are accessible when needed.

Methods:

* System maintenance
* Software updates
* Backups

---

## 2. Data States

### Processing (Data in Use)

Data being actively used or processed (e.g., database updates).

### Storage (Data at Rest)

Data stored on devices like HDDs, SSDs, or USB drives.

### Transmission (Data in Transit)

Data moving between systems over a network.

---

## 3. Security Measures

### Awareness, Training, and Education

Helps users understand threats and safe practices.

### Technology

Hardware/software security tools like:

* Firewalls
* Security monitoring systems

### Policies and Procedures

Organizational rules and guidelines such as:

* Incident response plans
* Security policies
* Best practice procedures


---

## Types of Attackers

### Cybercriminals

Individuals seeking financial gain through cyber attacks.

### Hacktivists

Attackers motivated by political or social causes.

### Nation-State Actors

Government-sponsored groups conducting cyber operations.

### Script Kiddies

Individuals using existing tools without deep technical knowledge.

### Insider Threats

Employees or trusted individuals who misuse access privileges.

---

## Internal and External Threats

### Internal Threats

Threats originating from within the organization.

Examples:

* Malicious employees
* Negligent users
* Accidental data exposure

### External Threats

Threats originating outside the organization.

Examples:

* Hackers
* Malware attacks
* Phishing campaigns
* Ransomware operators

---

## Cyber Warfare

Cyber warfare involves the use of cyber attacks by nation-states or organized groups to disrupt, damage, or gain intelligence from another country's systems and infrastructure.

Common targets include:

* Government networks
* Critical infrastructure
* Financial institutions
* Communication systems

---

## Key Takeaways

* Cybersecurity protects people, processes, and technology.
* Personal data is valuable to many organizations and threat actors.
* Businesses manage both traditional and modern data sources.
* The Cybersecurity Cube provides a framework for understanding security.
* Threats can originate from both internal and external sources.
* Cyber warfare represents one of the most advanced forms of cyber conflict.
